# Naming V2 — Abstract / Tech-Forward Style

Following the Convex / Notion / Linear / Clerk school: one word, short, abstract, conceptual, sounds like a global SaaS company.

---

| # | Name | Why it works |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | **Alto** | Musical/apex term — "high" in Spanish. Short, vowel-rich, memorable, feels premium. |
| 2 | **Tempo** | Pace and rhythm. Works for operations software. Musical, universal, easy to say globally. |
| 3 | **Pivot** | Strategic turning point. Startup vocabulary. Evokes agility and decision-making. |
| 4 | **Nova** | Astronomical — a sudden bright star. Short, modern, optimistic, recognizable. |
| 5 | **Summit** | Peak achievement. Business ambition metaphor. Feels enterprise-grade. |
| 6 | **Settle** | Means "to resolve" and "to make whole." Calm, trustworthy, verb-name. |
| 7 | **Fuse** | Joining things together. Implies integration and connection. Short, strong. |
| 8 | **Prime** | Best state, first position. Mathematical (prime numbers). Premium connotation. |
| 9 | **Flux** | Continuous change. Perfect for dynamic operations data. Tech-native feel. |
| 10 | **Crisp** | Clean, precise, high-quality. Sensory word that implies good UX. Distinctive. |

---

## Notes

- All are one word, 4–6 letters.
- None reference auto, Africa, or native languages.
- All are spellable on a first listen.
- All have plausible `.com` availability (short, abstract, not trademark-heavy).
- Top picks for "sounds like a real YC company": **Alto**, **Nova**, **Summit**, **Crisp**, **Flux**.
